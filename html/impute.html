Imputation attempts to solve the problem of filling in missing values in in the data frame. Imputing the mean value is almost never appropriate, since it disregards the underlying correlation structure. Other common methods are, for example, kNN (K-nearest neighbors), FKM (fuzzy K-means), SVD (singular value decomposition), bPCA (Bayesian principal component analysis), and MICE (multiple imputations by chained equations). MICE is much more complex and performs well with small datasets, but inconsistently with larger ones. Perhaps, bPCA and FKM are better performers all around (source).

The easiest method may be to simply drop examples with missing values, although this may be inappropriate since it may delete important data.
